We believe differently

According to some climate scientists, human activity is the primary cause of climate change. They contend that the only way to mitigate climate change is to transition to a sustainable economy based on renewable resources, such as sunlight.

Standing against their evidence for human-caused climate change is a deliberate act. Deciding not to reduce one’s carbon footprint is also a deliberate act. Opposing government regulations that can lower the carbon emissions of Western countries is a deliberate act as well.

If we maintain that climate change is either not occurring or not human-caused, we must take responsibility for our choices by signing the following pledge:

Take the Pledge

The Pledge

“I believe that climate change is a problem that neither deserves our attention nor warrants immediate action. I recognize that, according to what climate scientists claim is established science, the decision I make today can have irreversible consequences for the environment. However, I want to go on the record with my decision and, in doing so, take responsibility for what happens in the coming decades. I feel sufficiently confident about my anti-climate change beliefs to sign this pledge for future generations to see.”

The names of the signers will be unsealed in 2050. At that time the debate will be over!
